Difficulty Definitions

Normal Rigging

Standard controlled lowering with ropes. Used when limbs or wood need to be lowered safely, but the setup is routine.

Technical Rigging

Complex rigging, negative blocking, limited tie-in options, heavy pieces over targets, or situations requiring slower high-risk rope work.

Near Structure/Fence

The tree is near a house, fence, shed, driveway, or other property feature, but the risk is manageable with standard care.

High-Risk Structure/Fence

The tree or major limbs are directly over a structure, or a mistake would likely cause expensive damage.

Dead/Brittle

Compromised wood, dead limbs, brittle canopy, hollow areas, or unsafe tie-in/rigging concerns.

Poor Drop Zone

Limited landing area, obstacles below, tight yard, landscaping, fences, or other restrictions that slow production.

Bad Access / Long Drag

Difficult machine access, long hand-carry/drag distance, tight gate, or limited ability to stage equipment close to the work.

Steep Terrain

Sloped or unstable terrain that slows cleanup, machine work, climbing, dragging, or hauling.

Heavy Cleanup / Wood Volume

Large amount of brush, limbs, debris, or cleanup time beyond a normal removal.

XL Logs / Oversized Wood

Large diameter wood, usually over 32 inches, requiring extra machine time, cutting, loading, or handling.
